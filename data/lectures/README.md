### Dentoalveolar Surgery — Key Principles

Indications for extraction include:
- Non-restorable caries
- Advanced periodontal disease
- Orthodontic need
- Periapical pathology unresponsive to endodontic treatment
- Impacted teeth causing pathology

Contraindications (relative):
- Recent myocardial infarction (within 6 months)
- Bisphosphonate therapy — MRONJ risk
- Poorly controlled diabetes (HbA1c > 9%)
- Active radiation therapy to the head and neck

Anatomical considerations:
- Inferior alveolar nerve (IAN) runs in the mandibular canal
- Mental nerve exits at the mental foramen (between premolars)
- Maxillary sinus proximity to upper posterior roots
- Lingual nerve at risk during lower third molar surgery
